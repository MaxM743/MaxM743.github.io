Chladni Plate

Mathematics

Considering a square plate of dimension $a\times b$ constrained at the center forced to vibrate with a speaker producing a soung at a given frequency. A thin powder or sand is dispatched on its surface. Recalling the wave equation in two dimensions: $$c^2\nabla^2u=\dfrac{\partial^2}{\partial t^2}u$$ Using boundary conditions $u(\pm \frac{a}{2}, y)=u(x, \pm \frac{b}{2})=0$ coming from the fact that the sand cannot move at the edges of the plate, we can show that the zeros of the standing wave are of the form: $$u_{mn}(x, y)=\sin\left(\dfrac{m\pi x}{a}\right)\sin\left(\dfrac{n\pi y}{b}\right) - \sin\left(\dfrac{n\pi x}{a}\right)\sin\left(\dfrac{m\pi y}{b}\right)$$ where $m, n$ are integers.
